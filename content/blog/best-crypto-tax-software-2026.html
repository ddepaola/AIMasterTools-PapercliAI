Best Crypto Tax Software 2026: Koinly vs CoinTracker vs TaxBit and More

Crypto taxes are genuinely complicated. The right software saves hours — and catches deductions most traders miss. Here's how the top tools compare.

Why Crypto Taxes Are More Complex Than Stock Taxes

With stocks, your broker generates a 1099-B. You import it into TurboTax. Done.

Crypto is different. Every trade, swap, or DeFi transaction is potentially a taxable event. If you traded ETH for SOL, that's a disposal of ETH — you owe tax on any gain, calculated in USD at the time of the trade. Multiply that by hundreds or thousands of transactions across multiple exchanges, wallets, and chains, and the record-keeping becomes a real problem.

The IRS expects you to track every transaction. Crypto tax software exists to automate that — pulling in your exchange history, calculating your gain/loss on each transaction, and generating the forms your accountant or tax software needs. Without it, you're doing this manually in a spreadsheet. No one does it well that way.

What to Look for in Crypto Tax Software

  • Exchange integrations — Does it support the exchanges and wallets you actually use? Coinbase and Binance are table stakes. Kraken, KuCoin, DeFi wallets (MetaMask, Phantom), and L2s (Arbitrum, Optimism) vary by tool.
  • Transaction limit — Free tiers almost always cap transactions. If you trade actively, make sure the paid tier covers your volume before buying.
  • Cost basis methods — FIFO, LIFO, HIFO, and specific ID are all valid. HIFO (highest in, first out) often minimizes gains — but not every tool supports it.
  • DeFi and NFT support — If you use DeFi protocols or traded NFTs, this matters. Most tools handle it to some degree; quality varies significantly.
  • Tax-loss harvesting — Some tools identify unrealized losses you can sell before year-end to offset gains. Useful if you're actively managing your tax position.
  • Report format — Do you need IRS Form 8949, Schedule D, or TurboTax/H&R Block integration? Verify the output format works for your filing method.

1. Koinly — Best Overall for Most Traders

Koinly

Free plan available Paid: from $49/year 700+ integrations DeFi supported

Koinly is the most widely used crypto tax tool and earns it. The import process is fast — connect via API or upload CSV — and the interface makes it easy to review transactions and catch errors before generating reports. It supports over 700 exchanges, wallets, and blockchains, including most DeFi protocols and NFT activity.

The free plan lets you import all your data and preview your tax position, but you need a paid plan to download the actual tax reports. Paid plans start at $49/year for up to 100 transactions, with higher tiers for active traders. The Hodler plan at $99/year covers 1,000 transactions — which covers most retail traders.

HIFO cost basis is supported, TurboTax and H&R Block integration works well, and customer support is responsive. For most traders choosing their first (or only) crypto tax tool, Koinly is the default recommendation.

  • Best for: Most retail traders; particularly strong on exchange variety and ease of use
  • Free tier: Import + preview only; reports require paid plan
  • Watch out for: DeFi categorization sometimes needs manual review

2. CoinTracker — Best TurboTax Integration

CoinTracker

Free (up to 25 transactions) Paid: from $59/year 500+ integrations Portfolio tracking included

CoinTracker's standout feature is its TurboTax partnership — deeper integration than any other tool in this list. If you file with TurboTax and want the smoothest possible experience, CoinTracker is worth the extra cost. The automatic import for Coinbase transactions is particularly polished; Coinbase invested in CoinTracker and the integration shows.

Beyond taxes, CoinTracker functions as a full portfolio tracker. Real-time P&L, performance vs. benchmarks, and asset allocation views are all included. If you want one tool that handles both day-to-day portfolio tracking and year-end tax reporting, CoinTracker does both well.

The free plan covers only 25 transactions, which is effectively unusable for anyone beyond casual holders. Paid plans start at $59/year for 100 transactions.

  • Best for: TurboTax users; Coinbase-heavy portfolios; people who want portfolio tracking built in
  • Free tier: 25 transactions only — functional for very casual holders
  • Watch out for: Fewer DeFi integrations than Koinly; pricier at higher transaction volumes

3. TaxBit — Best for US Institutional and High-Volume Users

TaxBit

Free (basic reporting) Individual plans from $50/year Enterprise tier available

TaxBit started as the institutional-grade crypto tax solution and has expanded to individual users. It's backed by investors including Paradigm and Haun Ventures and has partnerships with major exchanges including Coinbase, Gemini, and FTX (for historical recovery). The software is built with CPA-level accuracy in mind.

For individual traders with high volume or complex situations (lots of DeFi, NFTs, yield farming, staking rewards), TaxBit's thoroughness is worth the price premium over Koinly. The UI is less polished than competitors but the underlying calculation engine is rock-solid.

For most retail traders, TaxBit is more than needed. Where it shines is for professionals, high-net-worth individuals, or anyone who wants a defensible audit trail with institutional-quality methodology.

  • Best for: High-volume traders; complex DeFi/NFT situations; institutional users
  • Watch out for: More complex interface; pricing scales steeply at high volumes

4. ZenLedger — Best for CPAs and Accountants

ZenLedger

Free (up to 25 transactions) Paid: from $49/year 400+ integrations CPA portal available

ZenLedger has a strong CPA-facing product — a portal that lets accountants manage multiple client accounts and review crypto transactions in a format designed for tax professionals. If you work with an accountant who handles your crypto taxes, asking them if they use ZenLedger might save you time and money compared to exporting from another tool.

For self-filers, ZenLedger is competent but doesn't clearly outperform Koinly on any dimension except the CPA workflow. It supports all major exchanges and generates the right forms (8949, TurboTax export). The interface has improved significantly over the past two years.

  • Best for: Anyone working with a CPA who uses ZenLedger; professional accountants managing client crypto
  • Watch out for: Fewer integrations than Koinly; self-filers get more for same price elsewhere

Tax-Loss Harvesting: The Year-Round Opportunity

Crypto's volatility creates a tax-loss harvesting opportunity that doesn't exist as cleanly in stocks. Because crypto has no wash-sale rule (as of 2026), you can sell a losing position, realize the loss, and immediately buy back in. That loss offsets gains elsewhere in your portfolio.

The timing matters. You need to identify unrealized losses before year-end and execute the sale before December 31. Koinly, CoinTracker, and TaxBit all have harvest optimization features that flag your largest loss positions. Use them in October–November, not on December 31st when everyone else is doing it and spreads widen.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need crypto tax software if I only made a few trades?
If you made fewer than 25 transactions all on one exchange, you can probably handle it manually or use the free tier of Koinly/CoinTracker. If you crossed exchanges, received staking rewards, or used any DeFi protocol, software is worth it — the manual effort to get the cost basis right outweighs the software cost at any volume.
Is Koinly or CoinTracker better?
For most people, Koinly. It has more integrations, better DeFi support, and comparable pricing. CoinTracker wins if you file with TurboTax and want the smoothest import, or if you want built-in portfolio tracking year-round. Both are reliable; the choice is a marginal one for most traders.
What if I didn't report crypto taxes in previous years?
You can file amended returns (Form 1040-X) for prior years. The IRS has a voluntary disclosure path that typically results in better outcomes than being discovered. Speak with a CPA who specializes in crypto before filing. Most crypto tax software lets you import historical data and generate reports for prior years.
Does crypto tax software work for NFTs and DeFi?
Yes, though quality varies. Koinly and CryptoTaxCalculator have the strongest DeFi coverage. NFT transactions — including minting, buying, selling, and gas fees — are supported by all major tools but may require manual review for edge cases. Always spot-check a sample of your DeFi transactions before submitting.
