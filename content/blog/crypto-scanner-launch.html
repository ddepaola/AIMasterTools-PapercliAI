What It Actually Does

The scanner doesn't just watch price. It watches everything at once.

At any given moment, our system is simultaneously analyzing RSI momentum divergences, volume spike patterns, cross-exchange price discrepancies, and behavioral patterns in the data — comparing what it sees against thousands of historical setups to assess signal strength before an alert ever fires.

Why We Built It This Way

Most signal tools are black boxes. You get an alert, you don't know why it fired, and you have no way to evaluate whether to trust it.

We took a different approach. Every signal that comes out of our scanner is explainable. The confidence score isn't arbitrary — it reflects how many of our detection layers are in agreement, weighted by each indicator's historical accuracy for that specific asset class. When three or four independent signals converge on the same setup simultaneously, that's when the alert fires.

This matters because it changes how you use the tool. You're not blindly following alerts. You understand what the system saw and why it flagged it. That makes you a better trader, not just a more active one.

We also built the scanner to run continuously without supervision. Markets don't stop at 5pm. A high-confidence setup at 3am on a Tuesday is just as real as one during peak hours. The scanner is always watching so you don't have to be.

The Technology Behind It

The scanner is built on Python with a live web dashboard served locally or via VPS. It connects directly to exchange APIs for real-time OHLCV data, processing hundreds of data points per second across multiple trading pairs.

Signal generation uses a multi-layer detection architecture — no single indicator triggers an alert. We require confluence across multiple independent signals before the confidence score reaches the firing threshold. This dramatically reduces false positives compared to single-indicator screeners.

All signals are logged in structured formats (CSV and JSONL) from day one, enabling full backtesting and strategy validation. Historical signal performance is available to Pro subscribers so you can evaluate the track record before making any trading decisions.
